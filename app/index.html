YUNIYA KAWAMURA, Fashion-ology

Similar to other countries, throughout history, dress standards and norms in Iran have changed alongside the country’s power and government transformations.

“Perhaps the biggest struggle in Iranian fashion history has been the struggle between the old and the new. Iranians have notoriously been fashion innovators trying to balance expectations of the different tastes in this vast country”

The History of Fashion in Iran

Timeline

250 BC - 224 AD

ACHAEMENIDS EMPIRE

Art forms, culture and religious inspired from different ancient Middle Easter people and combined it with one.

10th CENTURY

SALJOOGHI

There was a high influenced of Turkey culture and people trying to get rid of it.

17th CENTURY

SAFAVID

It was a great cultural centre. Painting, metalwork, textiles and carpets were in the heights of perfection. Art was devoted to celebrate the glories of the earlier Iranian kingdom.

1750 - 1779

ZANDIEH

A period of transition from Safavid to Qajar.

1785 - 1925

QAJAR

Great influenced from European in the arts among the royalty and noble classes on Persian culture. It can be seen from the painting, from the used of oil and the adoption of realism that was used by Qajar artists.

1925

PAHLAVI

Still highly influenced by European style. There was a big support from the government to follow European style. It lead to a ban on the chador.

1970s

PAHLAVI

The streets of Iran looked like Paris/London as they really followed western fashion trends.

1979

REVOLUTION

The changes of politic leadership changed the policy of clothing for women. It made them force to wear hijab.

1990

POST REVOLUTION

People started to had protests and movements to encourage the government for lenient in women’s clothing.

20th CENTURY

NOW...

People start to dress more fashionably. Colors start to integrate in the fashion. Fashion industry start to grow as well.
