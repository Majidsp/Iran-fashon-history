1925 - PAHLAVI

The clothing of Persians during the early years of the Pahlavi dynasty was generally similar to that of the Qajar period, reecting dierences among tribes, villages, and regions, as well as among classes.

Many Persian men who had traveled abroad had begun to adopt European dress, wearing suits, neckties, and bow ties.
September 1928 the cabinet resolved that all male Persians dress uniformly in Western style.

Reza Shah, Iranian leader at that time, limit the wearing of Hijab. For him wearing Hijab will limit the country’’s modernization to encourage european culture.
M. Reza Pahlavi, the next Iran’s leader, removed the band of the chador. For some women veil was a sign of virtue; protection and respect.

Maghnaeh— This is a large piece of fabric, sewn slightly different than the basic scarf, covering the head, hair and shoulders.

This type of covering has been worn with a manteau as the main form of hijab in Iran since 1981. It is a common part of the uniform expected to be worn by female students in Iran.

Fashion industry in Iran used to be something underground and hard to detect. Nowdays government start to allowing fashion industry to be more in public eyes. But it still strict under the government’s watch. All the fashion shows, clothes produced and colors used still must be under the rules of the government. Turning the classic hijab into something more Chic.

Transforming “manteau “ to a long loose coat into fashionable jacket. With a colorful loose scarf “roo-sari,” or “shawl” and some accesories such as belt and high heel shoes.
